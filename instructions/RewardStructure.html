How do I know which image to choose?


In each round, one image will reward you whilst the other will lower your score. Which image does which is the same for all players in the group.


In the game, the 60 rounds are split into a number of 'blocks'. Within each block, both images will give you wins and losses, but one image will give you more wins than the other.

However, this will switch multiple times across the game, such that the 'better' image will become the 'worse' image and vice versa.


For example, whilst it may be better overall to choose the 'Green' image than the 'Blue' image in one block, in the next block it will be the other way round.



This is a very important aspect of the game, because if the image gave you the same outcome everytime in each block, you would always know which one to pick and when exactly the reversals happen!


The challenge of the game is that when and how often the switches take place are unknown to you.

You should therefore pay attention to the feedback and adjust your choices accordingly.


Over the course of the game, you may choose to either trust your own learning experience through trial and error and/or take decisions from others into consideration, as some of the other players might learn faster than others.


After completing 60 rounds, the total number of points you earned will be displayed along with your bonus, after which you can subsequently complete the questionnaires.


Remember the more points you score, the higher your bonus payment is!
